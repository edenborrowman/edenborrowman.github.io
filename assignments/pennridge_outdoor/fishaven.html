The Legend of the Bear Lake Monster

Deep in the waters of Idaho's Bear Lake, lurkes a prehistoric creature, unlike anything known to modern man, or at least that's what legend claims. Reputed to be reptilian in shape, sixteen feet long with a cream-colored serpentâ€™s body, short but quick legs, and powerful jaws, early settlers claimed it was as comfortable stalking its prey on land as in the water. For decades the tales of the creature from the Rocky Mountain deep have thrilled and terrified local residents.

The first reports of the monster date to the earliest Mormon pioneer settlers. They were told about the the creature from their Native American neighbors, and records seem to indicate that the tales were taken seriously. They even reported the tale back to church headquarters in Salt Lake City, and church leaders who visited the valley were eager to see the banks of the lake to look for signs of the creature. Joseph C. Rich, a prominent citizen, claimed to have seen the beast gliding through the cold, clear water, a story he later recanted when questioned by church leadership.

Today, the legend of the monster serves as a fun campfire story and a tourist attraction. Bear Lake Monster souvenirs can be purchased up and down the shores of the lake during the summer season and every year, new generations are pulled into the fasc 3inating tale of the reclusive creature.
