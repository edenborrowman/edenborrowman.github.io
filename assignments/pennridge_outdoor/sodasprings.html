Sleep Outdoors

Willow Flat is a popular campground, located on the banks of the Cub River, in southeastern Idaho. It sits in a pretty valley, surrounded by scenic mountain views, at an elevation of 6,300 feet. Visitors enjoy fishing the river and exploring numerous forested trails.

Visitors are presented with numerous opportunities to enjoy the campground. On the banks of the Cub River, Willow Flat has ample fishing with wild cutthroad and hatchery rainbow trout. Several trailheads originate at the campground, including the popular and easy hike to Sam Parkinson Spring. Other well-loved trails include the Highline Trail, the North Fork of St Charles Creek trail and the Cub river Guard Station trail. Dirt biking, mountain biking, and horseback riding are other popular recreation choices at Willow Flat.

The campground itself is large and has excellent facilities, including picnic shelters, hook ups for RVs, tent camping sites, toilets, drinking water, an ampitheater with bleacher seating and a cement platform, and secluded, quiet areas. Elk and deer frequently visit the site, as well as many other forest animals, making Willow Flat an animal lover's paradise.

Tree huggers can enjoy the wide variety of spruce and other hardwood trees that provide most campsites with partial shade and beautiful vistas. The Cub River hiking trail is lined with dense groves of willow, a favorite food of the local Moose population.

This popular campsite books quickly. For best selection, plan on reserving your site early. Popular dates, like the 4th of July, can book as far as a year in advance. Plan your trip accordingly.
