Ice Fishing Extravaganza

Looking for a way to experience Southeastern Idaho's majesty in the middle of the winter? Fortunately there's a time-honored activity that will let you enjoy nature, and with a bit of luck, bring home dinner. It's ice fishing, an activity that promises fun for the whole family.

Even if you are new to the sport, ice fishing is inexpensive and easy and if you already fish, you probably own most of the basic gear you need for a successful outing. Adding an auger, a chair, and plenty of cold weather clothing will prepare you for a memorable trip. You can even bring a cooler filled with your favorite foods, a camping stove, and a fishing tent to make it a veritable off-season tailgating party.

When ice fishing season starts in Southeastern Idaho, as well as how long it lasts, will vary year by year. Conditions can change quickly with fluxuating temperatures, so be sure to exercise caution before heading out on the ice. With a little patience, you can expect to see rainbow trout, cutthroats, yellow perch, kokanee, lake trout, and a unique idaho fish called the cisco. Resivoirs are stocked each fall, making winter a prime time to land a fantastic catch.

Here are some of the top fishing sites, ranked by Pennridge Outdoor anglers:

1. Bear lake
2. Chesterfield Resivoir
3. Blackfoot Resivoir
4. Montpelier Resivoir
5. Pocatello's Urban Fisheries
