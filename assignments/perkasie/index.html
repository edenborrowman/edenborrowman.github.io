WELCOME TO PERKASIE

Perkasie is located in Bucks County, Pennsylvania and is situated approximately 30 miles north of Philadelphia, midway between Philadelphia and Allentown. A small, historic borough, Perkasie is the perfect place to get off the beaten path. Home to many award-winning independent restaurants, unique shopping opportunities including clothing boutiques, antiques shops, and locan and organic produce and meat vendors. Our italian market is the finest this side of Philly and the farmer's market is consistently voted the best in Buck's County. You can scratch a sweet tooth with homestyle premium ice cream at the Hickory Stick or Sundae School and the water ice, Philadelphia's famous frozen treat, is alway cold at Rita's. Our antique car culture is second to none with two world-renound restoration shops within borough limits and a world-class car show every forth of July.

For history lovers, Perkasie is a paradise, home of two historic covered bridges, the Pearl S.Buck house, and Perkasie Park, a resort that maintains its yesteryear charm.

Visitors can enjoy strolling down historic 5th street or enjoying the Perkasie trail, following the slow East branch of Perkiomen Creek. The five-mile trail is paved, shaded, and beautiful and connects with East Rockhill Township to the East and Sellersville to the West.

On behalf of the Perkasie Borough Chamber of Commerce and the appointed officials of Perkasie Borough, we wish to extend a very cordial welcome. We hope that you will find our pleasant community a great place to live.

THE PERKASIE CHAMBER OF COMMERCE

The Perkasie Borough Chamber of Commerce, established in 1937, works to promote the business intrests and community development of Perkasie, Pennsylvania, a borough in Upper Bucks County, about 25 miles north of Philadelphia.

Working with business owners, the borough council, and concerned citizens, we strive to expand economic opportunities for entrepreneurs and access to goods and services for the citizens of Perkasie. Our outreach has helped build our community for almost a century and the Perkasie Promise scholarship, funded by Chamber of Commerce member businesses, has helped send oveer 50 Pennridge High School graduates to college to study business, accounting, or economics.
